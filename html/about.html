About FCR Reviews

Video games have been a part of my life for as long as I can remember. Some of my earliest childhood memories are centered around playing games with my brother, which allowed us to bridge our four year age gap and connect over games like Crazy Taxi, Star Wars Battlefront, Pokemon, and Super Mario. As we grew up, we gravitated towards more single-player games and started fighting over who got to use the family desktop computer and eventually over who got to play Call of Duty after school. But once we got past our high school years, video games became something that we could bond over again as we updated each other from a distance on our recent favorites. Now that he has started his own family and has two little kiddos, we both get to share our love of video games with them. The connection and joy I have received from playing and sharing games with those close to me is what inspired me to share this experience with friends, and now even strangers. While video games are often seen as "childish" or "nerdy", I believe that they have so much to offer us, even as adults. Whether it's an escape from our daily grind, an opportunity to explore meaningful stories and learn more about ourselves, or a way to connect with friends from a distance, there is a game out there for everyone. And if I can connect even one person to a game that resonates with them, whether they were searching for it or not, then it's worth sharing.
