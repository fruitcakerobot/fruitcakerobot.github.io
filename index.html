BLOODLETTER: First Impression

BLOODLETTER is a roguelite deckbuilder where you play as a medieval barber surgeon tasked with protecting villagers from ancient evils, using dubious healing methods. As someone who is newer to deckbuilders, the day and night cycles that are introduced feel very comfortable as a gameplay loop. While the core mechanics seem simple at first, you quickly have to evolve your strategies to adapt to your growing deck. Maybe it was just my lack of experience with this genre, but the first entity, on easy mode, took me almost two hours to complete. What worked for me early on, quickly went out the window, as I struggled to maintain purity levels for the villagers. There may be some tweaking needed with balancing, as, again, this was on the easy level, but truthfully, I enjoyed the challenge. It requires your attention right out of the gate, and the strategies you learn can be applied to future runs, which adds to this game's replayability. On top of the core gameplay, the art style is gorgeous. The character designs are unique and add to the overall theme of the game. However, my favorite discoveries came when I received new cards for my deck. If the developers haven't considered merch yet, they should, and I will be buying immediately. All in all, my first impression of BLOODLETTER is an 8/10. I can't wait to dive back in!

The Last Gas Station: Demo First Impression

The Last Gas Station is a cozy store management simulation game where you are running the last gas station in a world taken over by electric vehicles.

Pepper Grinder: First Impression

Pepper Grinder is 2D action platformer with a unique twist. Rather than jumping from platform to platform, you move through the levels using a drill to dive in and out of the ground, taking down enemies along the way.
